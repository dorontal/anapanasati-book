## Ānāpānassati Instructions 

### Common Guidelines
* Ideally, breathe both in and out through the nose. If that's not possible or easy, breathe in through the nose and out through the mouth. As a last resort, breathe both in and out through the mouth.
* If thoughts arise, and you notice them, congratulate yourself for having noticed them and for having reconnected with your awareness, then gently resume keeping track of whether you're breathing in or out and whatever it is you are tracking. If you notice that the thoughts are wholesome, congratulate yourself for the wholesomeness of your thoughts as well. When you return to long breathing in this way, do so in a relaxed manner, not abruptly, not forcing the mind onto the breath in any way.
* If too many thoughts, use the Ajahn Chah trick REF.
* Never stumble into the next step. Sometimes you find yourself there naturally, without having intended to move on yet - attention drifts forward on its own. It's better to first return to the step you're at, and only move into the next step through intention, not by drifting onto it.
