#### Step 3 – Experiencing the Whole Body

>
> '<span style="color:black;">_sabbakāyapaṭisaṁvedī</span> <span style="color:green;"> assasissāmī</span>’ti <span style="color:blueviolet;">sikkhati</span>, ‘<span style="color:black;">sabbakāyapaṭisaṁvedī</span> <span style="color:red;">passasissāmī</span>’ti <span style="color:blueviolet;">sikkhati</span>;_

##### How to Practice
* Continuously keep track of whether you're breathing in or out. 
* Stop controlling breath length – let the body breathe on its own; let it automatically control breath length.
* On each in-breath  and out-breath, tune in to sensations in the body – anywhere in the body where they come up – visualizing the river of flowing sensations. Include all sensations, even pain or tension, within this field of awareness, not isolating, not focusing on any single part of the body but taking all sensations together.
* Sense the whole body breathing. Sense the breathing throughout the body as a unified process. You may visualize oxygen energizing every cell in the body and the out-breath expelling the CO2 that cells don't need.
* Notice how the whole body moves with each breath. These can be subtle micro-movements or all kinds of other motions that may occur.

##### Signs of Maturation
* The body is experienced as a continuous, unified presence rather than fragmented parts.
* Discomfort, tension or pain is noticed but no longer monopolizes attention.
* Attention remains steady across the entire body field.

##### Common misunderstandings
* By this step the meditator has been sitting for a while and pain might arise more strongly as time goes by. Pain easily drives other hindrances such as sensual desire (to be pain free) or ill will (toward the pain) or restlessness (impatience for the pain to go away). If pain arrives more strongly as time goes by in this step, try to include it in the whole body stream of sensation and maintain awareness of the whole body. However, don’t let pain in step 3 prevent you from moving into step 4 — the calming of bodily formation in step 4 may help with the pain.
