## Preparation
Before starting formal meditation practice, it's essential to spend some time establishing conditions that support stability and continuity of attention. When you build a house, the stronger the foundation, the more likely it is to withstand a storm. In the same way, we build a strong foundation for each meditation session so that it is not easily toppled.

### 1 – Posture
A good posture for meditation balances alertness and uprightness with ease and relaxation. Some muscular effort is always needed to keep the body aligned, but we aim to use as little muscle as possible - staying balanced without becoming rigid. Part of avoiding rigidity is recognizing that the body is never truly still - it is always in subtle motion, and this motion is tied to the breathing process. Seeing this clearly helps us notice the body's natural movement rather than resist it. The goal is not perfect posture but a position that lets the breath move naturally and awareness stay steady.

### 2 – Loving Kindness Meditation
There are numerous methods for loving kindness meditation, and every teacher interprets these methods differently. The specific method matters less than including two categories of beings in your well-wishes: (1) yourself, and (2) all beings everywhere. See Appendix REF for a description of some loving kindness methods you could use here.

This phase does not need to be lengthy - a couple of minutes can suffice. You may silently repeat a phrase, such as "May I be safe, happy and well" a few times, followed by repeating "May all beings everywhere be safe, happy and well." Whatever method you use, try to generate the feeling of the *brahmavihara* you're practicing. Loving kindness practice softens the heart, helping to reduce hindrances like aversion and restlessness.

### 3 – Motivation
The reasons for pursuing formal meditation practice are as numerous as its benefits - spiritual, mental health related, or otherwise - and every person has their own. Whatever yours are, bring them to mind by silently asking yourself, "Why am I doing this?" This helps reduce the hindrance of doubt, gives the mind clearer direction from the start, and is encouraging.

### 4 – [Optional] Plan the Session
Check in with your mental state and try to tweak your generic meditation method to fit it. There may be other reasons to tweak it too. It's useful to mentally sketch out a small plan for any variations, so fewer decisions have to be made during the meditation itself. For example, your plan might specify how long to spend on particular steps, or include a practice variation you're trying out.

### 5 – Present Moment Awareness
Abandon the past. Abandon the future. Shift your attention to the present moment. If you need a tool for this, you can try cycling between (a) telling yourself "I am aware that," then (b) paying attention to whatever arises - thought, touch, sound, odor, taste, or sight - and noticing how your awareness of it rises and falls.
