### Step 2 – Short Breath Awareness

> <span style="color:green;">either while breathing in short</span> <span style="color:blueviolet;">one understands</span> '<span style="color:limegreen;">I breathe in short</span>', <span style="color:red;">or while breathing out short</span> <span style="color:blueviolet;">one understands</span> '<span style="color:darkorange;">I breathe out short</span>';
>
> _<span style="color:green;">rassaṁ vā assasanto</span> ‘<span style="color:limegreen;">rassaṁ assasāmī</span>’ti <span style="color:blueviolet;">pajānāti</span>, <span style="color:red;">rassaṁ vā passasanto</span> ‘<span style="color:darkorange;">rassaṁ passasāmī</span>’ti <span style="color:blueviolet;">pajānāti</span>;_

#### How to Practice
* Switch to shorter breaths, meaning each leg of the breath takes less time, without forcing or exaggeration. Switch to whatever shorter duration feels comfortable and natural, such that when the body returns to breathing on its own, at the end of this step, it is breathing roughly at the same breath length you settle on here.
* You may shorten the breath gradually or rapidly – whatever suis you.
* Gradually allow the body to breathe on its own. 

#### Signs of Maturation
* Each breath is shorter than it was in Step 1.
* The body and mind settle further and become more still.
* The body is breathing on its own.
* The breath may begin to lengthen after the body breathes on its own and if that happens, that's a strong sign we are done with this step.

#### Common misunderstandings
Some meditators report the breath as actually lengthening as the body and mind become more still. In Step 1, we need to make the breath longer than the body's natural breath. If we don't lengthen the breath enough in Step 1, then when we let the body breathe on its own at the end of this step, it actually lengthens and does not become short.
